\section{Polycrystalline Model}

\subsection{Hamiltonian}

The project focuses on a two-dimensional system of grains (crystallites) arranged on a hexagonal lattice structure. Each grain has an orientation angle, denoted by $\theta_i$, which represents its deviation from a fixed reference orientation. This configuration corresponds to a graphene sheet placed on a hexagonal substrate.

In graphene, grain boundaries are known to consist of periodic arrays of dislocations whose elastic interactions determine the energetic cost of misorientation between neighboring crystallites \cite{Yazyev2010}. The distribution of grain orientations is governed by two competing interactions: the substrate coupling \cite{Rogge2015}, which tends to align the grains with the substrate, and the grain boundary energy, which favors local alignment between neighboring grains. This interaction is expressed according to Read-Shockley model \cite{Read1950}. The total Hamiltonian $\mathcal{H}$ of the system can be written as:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{H} = \frac{\epsilon}{\rho} \sum_i \theta_i^2 + \frac{\gamma}{3\rho} \sum_i \sum_{j \in nn_i} \left|\Delta \theta_{ij}\right| \left(A - \log\left|\Delta \theta_{ij}\right|\right)
    \label{eq:hamiltonian}
\end{equation}

Where $\epsilon$ is the substrate coupling strength, $\gamma$ is the grain boundary energy coefficient, $\rho$ is the grain density and $A$ is a constant related to the material properties. The term $\Delta \theta_{ij} = \theta_i - \theta_j$ represents the difference in orientation angles between neighboring grains $i$ and $j$, with the summation over $nn_i$ indicating that we only consider nearest neighbors. From now, $\rho$ and $A$ are set to $1$ for simplicity.

\subsection{Observables}

We define several observables to characterize the phases in the system as described in the KTHNY theory \cite{ Nelson1978,Nelson1979,Kosterlitz1973,Young1979}. To characterize the orientational order of the system, the local bond-orientational order parameter $\psi_6(\mathbf{r}_i)$ for each grain $i$ is defined as:
\begin{equation}
    \psi_6(\mathbf{r}_i) = \frac{1}{n_i} \sum_{j \in nn_i} e^{i 6 \theta_{ij}}
\end{equation}

Where $n_i$ is the number of nearest neighbors of grain $i$, and $\theta_{ij}$ is the angle between a reference axis and the bond connecting grain $i$ to its neighbor $j$. This parameter captures the six-fold rotational symmetry characteristic of the hexagonal lattice structure. Here, a coarse-grained approximation is used. The local bond orientation of each grain is fully determined by its orientation angle $\theta_i$, so the local bond-orientational order parameter can be simplified to:
\begin{equation}
    \psi_6(\mathbf{r}_i) = e^{i 6 \theta_i}
\end{equation}

The bond-orientational correlation function $G_6(r)$ between grains separated by a distance $r$ is defined as:
\begin{equation}
    G_6(r) = \langle \psi_6(\mathbf{r}_i) \psi_6^*(\mathbf{r}_j) \rangle_{|\mathbf{r}_i - \mathbf{r}_j| = r}
\end{equation}

Where the average is taken over all pairs of grains $(i, j)$ separated by distance $r$. The behavior of $G_6(r)$ provides information about the orientational order of the system. In a hexatic phase, $G_6(r)$ is expected to decay as a power law:
\begin{equation}
    G_6(r) \sim r^{-\eta_6}
\end{equation}

Where $\eta_6$ is a critical exponent characterizing the decay of orientational correlations. At the transition between hexatic and disordered phases, $\eta_6$ reaches a universal value of $1/4$. In contrast, in a disordered phase, $G_6(r)$ decays exponentially:
\begin{equation}
    G_6(r) \sim e^{-r/\xi_6}
\end{equation}

Where $\xi_6$ is the orientational correlation length. In an ordered phase, $G_6(r)$ must be equal to a constant for large $r$, indicating long-range orientational order. In the limiting case $\gamma = 0$, grain orientations become independent, leading to an exact expression for $G_6(r)$ \cite{Ruslan2025}:
\begin{equation}
    G_6(r) = \exp\left(- 18 \rho \frac{k_B T}{\epsilon} \right)
    \label{eq:theoricG6}
\end{equation}

Finally, the susceptibility associated with the bond-orientational order parameter, $\chi_6$, is defined as \cite{Weber1995}:
\begin{equation}
    \chi_6 = N \beta \left( \left\langle |\Psi_6|^2 \right\rangle - \left\langle |\Psi_6| \right\rangle^2 \right)
\end{equation}

Where $N$ is the total number of grains in the system, $\beta = \frac{1}{k_B T}$ and $\Psi_6 = \frac{1}{N} \sum_i \psi_6(\mathbf{r}_i)$ is the global bond-orientational order parameter. This susceptibility measures the fluctuations in the bond-orientational order parameter. At the hexatic to disordered phase transition, $\chi_6$ is expected to diverge. In finite systems, this divergence manifests as a pronounced peak scaling with system size.
